**Subject:** Educational data partnership — non-commercial U.S. drinking-water foundation

---

Hi EWG team,

I'm writing on behalf of the Water Awareness Foundation, a non-commercial educational organization publishing plain-English, fully-sourced reports on U.S. tap water at waterawarenessfoundation.com. We've built a per-ZIP-code report tool that surfaces utility-level contaminant findings — the kind of work that EWG's Tap Water Database has set the standard for over the past decade.

Like a lot of independent education projects, we'd love to be able to draw on EWG's contaminant-level data through a sanctioned channel rather than scraping or duplicating effort. A few things about us that may matter:

- **Non-commercial.** The foundation accepts no advertising, no affiliate revenue, and no funding from utilities, bottled water companies, or filter manufacturers. Full transparency disclosure at waterawarenessfoundation.com/transparency.
- **Attribution-first.** Any data we surface from EWG would be clearly credited inline, with deep links back to the EWG system page for the utility in question.
- **Editorial alignment.** Our editorial framing distinguishes EPA legal limits from EWG's health-protective guidelines exactly the way EWG does — including being explicit that "above guideline" is not a legal violation. We are not in the business of misrepresenting the data.
- **Reciprocal value.** Where it's useful, we're happy to drive readers and citations back to EWG's reports, give EWG editorial input on how its data is presented, and surface partnership credit on every page that uses EWG data.

We're open to whatever structure works on your side — a formal data license, a non-commercial educational use agreement, an API partnership, or something more informal. We'd also welcome the opportunity to discuss what *not* to do; we'd rather respect your boundaries than guess at them.

If there's someone better to route this to internally, please let us know. Happy to send a longer brief, get on a call, or share access to the in-progress site for review.

## Notes for the sender

- Send from a real foundation email address (`hello@waterawarenessfoundation.com` or similar). A `gmail.com` send will be filed as cold pitch and likely ignored.
- Attach: a one-pager PDF describing the foundation, its funding policy, and the proposed scope of EWG data use.
- If no response in 14 days, follow up with the EWG general counsel or the comms director found on ewg.org/about/staff.
- If response is "no": the next-best step is to scope a data license through SimpleLab (mytapscore.com), which sells utility-level water-quality data with an explicit licensing model. Cost is meaningful (low five-figures annually for full coverage).
